Government Schemes for Farmer-Marketer Direct Connect

Empowering Farmers by Connecting Them Directly to Markets

Government Schemes Overview

1. PM Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

The PM Kisan Samman Nidhi scheme is a direct income support initiative by the Indian government for farmers. Under this scheme, eligible farmers receive Rs. 6,000 per year in three equal installments. This financial support can be used to bridge the gap between the costs of farming and accessing direct markets.

  • Direct transfer of financial assistance to farmers' accounts.
  • Helps farmers invest in better farming practices and infrastructure.
  • Supports farmers' participation in farmer-marketer platforms and direct sales.

2. eNAM (National Agriculture Market)

eNAM is an online platform developed by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare to create a unified national market for agricultural commodities. It aims to remove middlemen, thereby increasing the efficiency of trading and empowering farmers to sell their produce directly to traders across the country.

  • Farmers can register on the platform and trade their produce directly with traders, bypassing traditional markets.
  • Access to transparent pricing and real-time market trends.
  • Promotes fair trading practices and helps increase farmers' incomes.

3. FPO (Farmer Producer Organizations)

Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) are groups formed by farmers to pool resources and produce together. These organizations act as a collective bargaining entity, improving farmers' ability to negotiate with marketers and buyers.

  • FPOs help small farmers gain access to large markets, thus enhancing their bargaining power.
  • Facilitates direct sale of farm produce to retailers and wholesale buyers.
  • Provides training, financial assistance, and infrastructure development to farmers through government subsidies and grants.

4. Agri Infrastructure Fund

The Agri Infrastructure Fund is a scheme to support the development of agri-business infrastructure. It provides long-term, low-interest loans for setting up warehouses, cold storage, logistics, and market linkages to ensure better access to markets and reduce post-harvest losses.

  • Supports investment in infrastructure for direct market access.
  • Helps in reducing post-harvest waste and increasing farmers' income.
  • Enhances farmers’ market competitiveness by improving storage and transportation facilities.

5. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

PMFBY is a crop insurance scheme that ensures farmers' crops are protected against natural calamities, pests, and diseases. This safety net enables farmers to focus on producing quality goods and encourages them to access direct market opportunities without fear of financial losses.

  • Offers coverage for losses due to natural disasters, pests, or diseases.
  • Farmers can focus on improving production and selling directly to markets.
  • Helps farmers maintain stable income and reduces dependency on middlemen.

6. Digital Agricultural Platforms

The Indian government is promoting digital platforms that connect farmers directly with buyers, including agri-tech startups and mobile applications. These platforms allow farmers to find market linkages and sell their produce without intermediaries.

  • Allows farmers to access real-time market information and prices.
  • Provides online platforms for farmers to connect with retailers, wholesalers, and processors.
  • Promotes transparency and efficiency in agricultural trade.

7. National Mission on Agricultural Extension and Technology (NMAET)

The National Mission on Agricultural Extension and Technology aims to improve farmers' awareness and accessibility to direct market opportunities by promoting the use of technology and modern farming techniques. It encourages farmers to adopt innovative solutions and better marketing strategies.

  • Enhances farmers’ understanding of modern agricultural techniques and market linkages.
  • Encourages digital tools and e-commerce platforms for direct farmer-market connections.
  • Supports the creation of market infrastructure and skill development in rural areas.
